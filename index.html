Shadow of Soul

SUMMARY

Platform: PC
Genre: action-adventure
Year: WIP
Engine: Unity
Role: Designer, writer and programmer
Team size: 1
Released: No

Shadow of Soul is a story-driven fantasy third person action-adventure game with gameplay inspired by old PS1 classics such as Metal Gear Solid, Dino Crisis, Silent Hill, Parasite Eve, Resident Evil, combining fast-paced both magical and firegun combat with level exploration and puzzle (environmental and logical) solving. Exploration also gives an additional insight into the game's world, lore and story.

The campaign spans over 16 episodes (of various size and length) and concentrates on two characters - Ian (protagonist) and Tony (Royal Guard Captain) as they seek a way to defeat Vaas, a powerful vampyre who threatens to resurrect The World Eater and use his power to bring his dead fiancee, Emily, back to life.

The story forces Ian and Tony to travel across many locations throughout a fantasy land (i.e. forgotten village of elves, ghost ship, underground chimeran laboratory) with some levels taking a more open-ended approach and giving player opportunity to complete sidequests, additional puzzles rewarded with valuable weapons and rare items and interact with occupying the world NPCs to learn more about the game's lore.

Gameplay shares the pace evenly between combat and puzzles. Our fighting methods consist of magic spells and firepower. Depending on the enemy, player might need to use a specific item to easier defeat his opponents (i.e. tear of light clears the black fog clearing our vision) as each foe has his own unique attacks and moves. Enemies are divided into tens of different types (zombies, zombie dogs, werewolves, vampyres, gargoyles, ravenwitches), each type belongs to one of several classes (undead, cursed, abominations, chimera). To proceed player will often have to solve a puzzle. I'm doing my best to make puzzles blend with the environment as much as possible and make them 'flow' nimbly with the rest of the gameplay and story - for example, one of the first puzzles requires player to revive a dead tree by rerouting a special mechanism and allowing the ravaged nature to soak a reviving potion; the tree then turns out to be the Ghost of the Forest; this friendly entity advises player as to what he needs to do next and gives background on a place it grows in.

The game began its life in November 2018 as a NES survival horror homebrew, but it quickly turned into a proper full game in Unity. Since I started working on this project, it came through many changes - from a 2D survival horror game with retro graphics the conception changed into a 3D action-adventure with focus on the anime-like multi-layered story taking away its survival horror roots.

The development continues steadily and currently the prototype showcasing the general concept is in the making. I've programmed inventory, some items (like health potion), basic TPP movement (walking, jumping and climbing ladder), basic AI (enemies patrol the area and attack player on sight), interactions with environment, two types of enemies (zombie and zombie dog - the latter can summon two more zombie dogs if its health drops low), shooting mechaniques with two weapons (revolver and shotgun), ammunition limit and handling and also general features like pausing and resuming game.

My Home Is My Castle

SUMMARY

Platform: PC
Genre: strategy
Year: 2016
Engine/Language: Unity
Role: Designer and programmer
Team size: 1
Released: No

My Home Is My Castle is a defense strategy game focusing on a tactical combat where player builts his own manually controlled cybertech defense system and fights off demons invading his constantly expanding, procedurally generated underground dungeons using made from scratch robots and traps.

Gameplay is divided into two parts - day (turn-based) and night (real-time). During the day, player plans his line of defense (places units, builds robots, sets traps, plans tactics), fixes the damage done overnight and manages the whole complex. Night is demon time - player defends his area and tries to conquer new grounds.

Each day dungeons grow procedurally making the game harder and harder as the area player must defend and conquer expands continuously. Over time our adversaries get stronger and smarter and in later parts of the game enemy generals start to appear who can organize their own armies and raise battles on their own. They may also take over and rule parts of the dungeons which will make it tougher to reclaim the areas that belong to the enemy.

The machines player fights with are made entirely from scratch by combining together various elements from many different types (similar to how space vehicles are created in Kerbal Space Program). Additionaly, player's creations can be programmed with visual conditional lists (i.e. if you see enemy, alarm other robots in nearest viccinity - instead of fighting your opponents), so only player's imagination limits what he can create - it's possible to make a fully automatic robots shooting rockets and flame throwers, but if player would rather set laser-activated booby traps he can do that, too.

I've learned a lot from this project - mostly that I shouldn't bite more than I can chew. While technologically the whole concept was relatively easily achievable with Unity (the prototype contained working procedural map generation, basic robot creating and spawning enemies), it turned out to put quite a lot of issues on the design part. I couldn't decide how exactly would player be able to control his area, all the randomness and freedom made it harder to design flow of the gameplay (should it mercilessly constantly increase the difficulty level or rather make a use of rubber-band techniques to adjust the toughness to player's situation), what UI would be best for the robot creation system. Because of that, the project was put on hold until I learn more about designing strategy games with such offbeat features I've had planned.

Wrath of Waters

SUMMARY

Platform: PC
Genre: RPG
Year: 2014
Engine/Language: Unreal Engine 4
Role: Programmer
Team size: 7
Released: No
Visit website (archived)

In April 2014 I joined an amateur group creating a fantasy RPG set in a near, post-apocalyptic future devastated by a terrible nature cataclism.

The game focused strongly on a multi-dimensional story with quite many moral choices throughout. We wanted to make a believable, immersive world that wouldn't be flat 'black and white', but would instead put player among characters where each one of them have their own not-so-evil and not-so-noble motives (similar to L.A. Noire), so all the moral choices player would have to make wouldn't be so obvious and would eventually put on the edge chances of survival of many innocent people living in a tough, hazardous world.

The team decided to use then-fresh Unreal Engine 4 to put their vision into reality. As a programmer, I contributed to the project by coding dialogue system, some elements of UI, inventory and combat. When I joined, Wrath of Waters have had been in the making for about 2 years already. After several months, in the fall of 2014 the project was put on hold as it grew a bit too much for such devoted, but small team.

Amnesia

SUMMARY

Platform: PC
Genre: point'n'click adventure
Year: 2009
Engine/Language: Blitz Basic 3D
Role: Designer, writer and programmer
Team size: 2
Released: Demo

My very first project with a final release planned was a classic point'n'click adventure. The development reached a fairly advanced stage - joined by a friendly 3D graphic, we've managed to make a demo of the game (described below) which was then sent to a couple of friends.

The game followed a plot similar to a Polish classic 'Jack Orlando', except it was set in a modern American city instead of 1930s. A detective wakes up in a shack on a wasteland with strong headache and amnesia. He doesn't remember how he got there or any of the recent events. Tracking back his last steps leads him to uncover a criminal mystery story of violence, theft and murder.

Several locations were made (shack, gas station, a few boards in a city) with a number of puzzles and a few NPCs the player could talk with. The demo version featured a basic inventory which allowed for pickin up, checking and using gathered items.

GBnest

SUMMARY

Platform: PC
Year: 2016
Engine/Language: C++, SFML
Role: Programmer
Team size: 1
Released: No, but finished

Since I got interested in computer science I always focused on software programming and had little knowledge of the hardware. I wished to learn more on how computers operate from the 'inside', how CPU processes data and commands, what's going on at that lower level. I decided that the best way to learn this stuff is by writing an emulator. I started with CHIP-8 and Intel 8080 emulators and moved to a bit more advanced machine.

Emulating GameBoy helped me to understand how consoles work on a low level. During the development which took about 3 days, I learned about CPU instructions (how they're constructed and processed), registers and flags (what they exist for and how they work), memory managament, communication between individual components, working on individual bits, how GameBoy handles sprites and how this pocket console is initialized.

Blood Remake

SUMMARY

Platform: PC
Year: 2018
Engine/Language: Unity
Role: Designer and programmer
Team size: 1
Released: No

Blood is my favourite 1990s FPS and I think it should get a good remake with modern graphics and reworked levels. Since we're probably not getting it, I spontaneously created my own remake of Blood in Unity.

In a few weeks I've coded FPS movement (running, sprinting, strafing, jumping, crouching), shooting mechaniques for flare gun (shooting projectiles, ammo, killing zombies) and also redesigned the first level of the game. I also added some sound effects like shooting sound, ambient background, flare sound and animations for flare gun (moving and shooting). Player can shoot one projectile at a time and he needs to wait a brief moment (less than a second) to shoot another one. The flare projectile burns out after a while and it sticks to other objects in a scene like it used to in the original.

Development was put on hold as I moved to working on Shadow of Soul.
